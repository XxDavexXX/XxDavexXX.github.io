Processors are the heart and brain of the computer, the most important part, so we must pay special attention to it when choosing our equipment. We are going to see how we should choose a processor and what the new generations of Intel offer us.

How does a processor work?



To know the characteristics of a processor we must look at several details. Intel is the majority manufacturer for its quality, so we will follow its nomenclature. Each processor comes with a number that indicates its performance after the well-known "Core i". The Core i3 is perfect for the simplest tasks: surfing the internet, office tools such as a word processor or a spreadsheet, etc.

The Core i5 can already perform more powerful tasks such as some video games or programs that require a certain graphic demand. Finally, we have the Core i7 that are indicated for more demanding tasks: high-demand video games, 4K streaming video or virtual reality, among others.

On the other hand, we have the number of cores, which will define the number of independent central processing units of each processor or chip. The most common is that we find equipment with 2, 4, 5, 8 and up to 10 cores. Some ranges can work with several processors at the same time, dividing the work between them, which is called Hyperthreading.

Along with the number of cores, we find the working frequency, which is the opening and closing speed of the transistors that make up the processor. The one provided by the manufacturer is always the operating frequency at which the chips will work most of the time.

Lastly, we should pay attention to the cache, a type of fast memory located next to the processor and which is used for the CPU to have all the necessary information, thereby gaining execution speed. In Intel processors we have a cache memory (SmartCaché) between 2 and 8 MB usually.Why change our processor or equipment As we see, processors offer much more than computing power. Number of Processors for Gaming







The processor number, which comes after the generation number, serves to differentiate between features within a processor family, including base clock speed, maximum frequency, cache size, number of cores and threads, support memory and much more. These numbers do not cross over between different families of processors. The Intel® Core™ i5-8400 processor has the following: Maximum frequency up to 4 GHz 6 cores and 6 threads 9 MB cache size The Intel® Core™ i5-8600K processor has the following: Maximum frequency up to 4.30 GHz 6 cores and 6 threads 9 MB cache size The Intel® Core™ i7-8700 processor has the following: Maximum frequency up to 4.60 GHz 6 cores and 12 threads 12 MB cache size

Gaming Processor Series Suffix







Although the family and generation indicate the performance level of a core CPU, its capabilities and specifications are described in greater detail in the Intel® CPU series names. The CPU series, indicated by the series suffix at the end of the CPU name, indicates the type of systems for which the processor is designed. A suffix, or lack thereof, indicates whether a CPU is designed for desktop, laptop, mobile, etc.

The following suffixes generally indicate that a CPU is ideal for gaming:

Without suffix or with suffix

S

CPUs that do not have a suffix or have an S suffix belong to the S series. These processors are made for desktop computers and offer a variety of options for various budgets and needs.

h

CPUs with an H as a suffix belong to the H-series, a series of powerful 4-laptop processors. Those looking for a good gaming CPU should also consider a few other suffixes.

k

A K suffix denotes an unlocked desktop processor that can be overclocked, while an “HK” (H + K) suffix indicates a high-powered, unlocked laptop processor that can be overclocked. Overclocking allows you to potentially achieve CPU performance beyond your specifications by tuning key system values.

F

The F suffix indicates a CPU that does not have integrated graphics. They must be combined with an external graphics card.

G.

The G suffix designates a CPU with additional integrated graphics built into it. For example, the Intel® Core™ i7-8809G processor in the Intel® NUC NUC8i7HVK includes Radeon™ RX Vega M GH graphics.

Other suffixes to consider:

x

An Intel® Core™ processor with an X or XE as a suffix indicates it belongs to the Intel® Core™ X series, a line designed for advanced creator workflows.5 These processors have high core counts for performance demands more extreme.6
